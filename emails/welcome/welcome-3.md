Last setup email. Then we're rolling.

Three ways to use this list. Pick the one that fits how you actually behave:

**1. Just read it.** That's it. You don't have to do anything. Sometimes the tip will stick in your head and surface next time the situation comes up — at the restaurant, the night before a flight, the morning after pickleball. That counts. That's the whole point.

**2. One thing, one week.** Don't try to do all seven. Pick one tip that hits, run it for the week, see what changes. If it works, keep it. If it doesn't, drop it without ceremony. Stack the keepers.

**3. Forward it.** If a tip's good, send it to the buddy you've been telling for a year that he should "do something." Way lower friction than another lecture from you over drinks.

A few things this list will NEVER do:

– Tell you to give up wine, steak, the boat, the restaurants, or the rest of the life you built. The whole point of optimizing is so you can keep enjoying it longer, not white-knuckle through a kale-only retirement.
– Sell you supplements through the email itself.
– Drop into hype mode. "TRANSFORM YOUR LIFE IN 7 DAYS" energy is for people who haven't lived 50 years yet.
– Pretend everything is fixable with one tip. It isn't. But the small stuff stacks, and most of you have never tried stacking it for real.
