What to Expect After Your Injection

It's completely normal to experience some discomfort at the injection site. PRP works by triggering your body's natural inflammatory response‚Äîthis is how healing begins. You may notice mild to moderate soreness, swelling, or bruising for the first few days. This is a sign that the treatment is working.

Most patients report that any discomfort peaks around 2-3 days post-injection and gradually improves from there. Full therapeutic benefits typically develop over 4-6 weeks as new tissue regeneration occurs.

Recovery Timeline

Days 1-3

Initial Recovery Phase

Rest the treated area. Mild to moderate soreness and swelling are expected. Ice may be applied for 15-20 minutes at a time if needed for comfort (wait 24 hours after injection). Avoid anti-inflammatory medications.

Days 4-7

Early Healing Phase

Discomfort should begin decreasing. Light daily activities can resume. Continue to avoid strenuous exercise or heavy lifting involving the treated area.

Weeks 2-3

Progressive Activity

Gradually increase activity level as tolerated. Light stretching and gentle range-of-motion exercises may be introduced. Listen to your body‚Äîsome soreness with activity is okay, but sharp pain is not.

Weeks 4-6

Tissue Regeneration

Most patients begin experiencing significant improvement during this phase. You may gradually return to normal exercise and activities. Full benefits continue to develop over the following weeks.

Do's and Don'ts

‚úì Do This

  • Rest the treated area for 48-72 hours
  • Stay well hydrated
  • Eat a nutritious, protein-rich diet
  • Use Tylenol (acetaminophen) if needed for pain
  • Apply ice after the first 24 hours if swelling occurs
  • Keep the injection site clean and dry
  • Get adequate sleep to support healing
  • Follow up as scheduled

‚úï Avoid This

  • NSAIDs (ibuprofen, Advil, Aleve, aspirin) for 2 weeks
  • Strenuous exercise for 1-2 weeks
  • Alcohol for 48 hours
  • Smoking or tobacco products
  • Hot tubs, saunas, or swimming pools for 48 hours
  • Massage directly on injection site for 1 week
  • Corticosteroid injections in the treated area

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Avoid Anti-Inflammatory Medications

NSAIDs (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs) can interfere with the healing process that PRP initiates. Please avoid the following for at least 2 weeks after your injection:

  • Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin)
  • Naproxen (Aleve)
  • Aspirin (except low-dose cardiac aspirin if prescribed)
  • Meloxicam, Celecoxib, and other prescription NSAIDs

Pain Management

Some discomfort is normal and expected‚Äîit means your body is responding to the treatment. For pain management, Tylenol (acetaminophen) is safe to use and will not interfere with healing.

If you experience significant discomfort, you may apply ice wrapped in a thin towel to the area for 15-20 minutes at a time, several times per day. Wait at least 24 hours after your injection before using ice.

If pain is severe or not improving after several days, please contact our office.
