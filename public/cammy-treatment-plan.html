Understanding Your Treatment

What Are These Medications?

BPC-157

BPC-157 is a small protein chain called a peptide. Your body makes a similar version in your stomach. Research shows it may help your body heal and repair tissues faster. It works on muscles, tendons, your gut, and other areas.

TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)

TB-500 is also a peptide that your body naturally makes. It plays a role in tissue repair and may help calm inflammation. Your provider has prescribed these two peptides together because they work well as a team.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to Give Your Injection

1
Wash your hands well with soap and water.
2
Clean the injection spot with an alcohol swab. Let it dry completely.
3
Pinch the skin on your belly (stay 2 inches away from your belly button) or the front of your thigh.
4
Insert the needle at a 45-degree angle (halfway between straight down and sideways).
5
Push the plunger slowly and steadily until all the medicine is in.
6
Pull the needle out and press gently with a clean cotton ball.
7
Put the used needle in your sharps container right away. Never reuse needles.

Tip: Rotate where you inject each time. This helps prevent skin irritation. Keep a simple log of where you inject.

FAQ

Common Questions

Where do I store my peptides?
Keep them in the refrigerator (not the freezer). Store away from light in the original packaging. Once mixed with water, use within 4-6 weeks. Check the date before each use.
Can I take this with food?
Yes, you can take it with or without food. Some people find it works a little better on an empty stomach, but this isn't required. Do what feels best for you.
What if I miss a dose?
Just take your next dose at the regular time. Do not double up to make up for it. Missing one dose won't ruin your progress. Just try to stay consistent.
Are there side effects?
Most people do very well. Some may notice a small red spot where they injected. This usually goes away quickly. Rarely, some people feel tired or have mild stomach upset. Let us know if anything feels off.
Can I exercise while on this?
Yes! Gentle movement can help healing. Listen to your body and don't push too hard if you're tired. As you start feeling better, you can slowly do more.
Why the 2-week break after 12 weeks?
The break allows your body's signaling systems to return to baseline. This helps make sure the peptides keep working well. Your provider will let you know if you should start another cycle.
Lifestyle Tips

Helping Your Body Heal

These simple habits can support your treatment and help your body heal faster.

  • Sleep: Try to get 7-9 hours each night. Your body does its best repair work while you sleep.
  • Water: Drink plenty of water each day. This helps flush out toxins and supports healing.
  • Food: Focus on whole foods like vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Try to limit sugar and processed foods.
  • Stress: Find ways to relax. Stress can slow down healing. Even a few minutes of deep breathing helps.
  • Movement: Gentle walking or stretching is great. Listen to your body and rest when needed.
