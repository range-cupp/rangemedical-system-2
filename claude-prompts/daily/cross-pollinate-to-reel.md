# Convert a daily tip into a reel script

Use this prompt when a daily tip landed (high opens, lots of replies, a "yes finally someone said this" moment) and you want to turn it into a reel. Drop into the Claude project that has `CLAUDE.md` loaded.

---

## Prompt

I have a daily tip that landed in email. Convert it into a reel script for @chriscupppppp.

**The email:**

```
[paste the tip here — subject + body]
```

**What's different about reel vs. email:**
- Reels are spoken — write for the ear, not the eye. Contractions. Sentence fragments OK. No semicolons.
- The first 1.5 seconds is everything. The hook has to land before he scrolls. The email's subject line is your starting raw material — not the answer.
- Reels can use a visual gag, a setting (gym, kitchen, clinic), or a face-cam shift. Note where one of these would help.
- Length: 25-45 seconds, max. If you can't say it that fast, cut harder.

**What I want from you:**
1. **The hook (first 1.5 seconds, ~5-10 words).** Stop-the-scroll level. Pain or curiosity. NEVER "Hey guys, today I want to talk about…"
2. **The setup (5-10 seconds).** Name the problem in his words. Use real avatar pain language ("you're 50, you wake up at 3am, your labs are normal, you feel like garbage").
3. **The reveal (5-15 seconds).** The actual tip. Specific. Concrete. Same content as the email's middle + action line, just spoken.
4. **The button (3-5 seconds).** A close that gives him permission to keep enjoying his life OR a sarcastic reframe of the standard advice. Optional CTA — usually NOT "DM me DAILY" on a content reel; let the bio do that work. Use "DM me DAILY" only if the reel is explicitly opt-in driven.

**Format your output:**
- Beat-by-beat with timestamp ranges.
- Mark `[ON CAMERA]`, `[CUT TO B-ROLL: …]`, or `[TEXT OVERLAY: …]` where relevant.
- Estimated total length at the bottom.
- One-line gut check: would Chris's avatar (45-55, Newport, Attia-reader) double-tap this? If not, what would push it over?

**Voice rules same as `CLAUDE.md`:**
- Sarcastic, funny, honest. Dry delivery on the heavy stuff.
- Mild-to-moderate swearing when it earns the laugh.
- No "wellness influencer" energy. No bro-science. No 101.
- Self-deprecation when it works. Lived experience > credentials.

Go.

---

## When this works best

- Tips that have a strong "everyone knows this is wrong but nobody says it" energy
- Tips with a single concrete action (good for B-roll: pour the magnesium powder, point at the lab order, hold up the ApoB request)
- Tips where the punchline is a reframe ("stop blaming melatonin")

## When to skip

- Tips that are heavy on context — more than 30 seconds of setup is a podcast, not a reel
- Tips that don't have a visual hook anywhere
- Tips that are pure list ("5 things to do tonight") — these die on Reels. Convert to one of the five instead.
