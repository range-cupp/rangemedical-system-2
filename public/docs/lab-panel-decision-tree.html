Why These Extra Markers Matter

Standard cholesterol panels miss the real story. Apo B tells you how many particles are carrying cholesterol into your artery walls — not just how much cholesterol is floating around. Lipoprotein(a) is genetic, can't be changed by diet, and is one of the strongest independent predictors of heart attack and stroke. Apo A-1 measures your body's ability to clear cholesterol out. These three markers together are what longevity doctors consider the gold standard for cardiovascular risk.

CRP-HS and Homocysteine catch silent inflammation — the kind that drives chronic disease before you feel anything. And a full vitamin/mineral panel (B12, folate, magnesium, iron, ferritin) reveals deficiencies that affect energy, sleep, recovery, and brain function.

3 Know These Three Markers (Elite Only)
Patients will ask about these. Here's what they are in plain English.

Apo B

Counts the number of particles that carry cholesterol into your artery walls. A better predictor of heart disease than LDL alone.

Think of it as: "How many trucks are delivering cholesterol to your arteries?"

Lipoprotein(a) — Lp(a)

A genetic marker. Can't be changed by diet or exercise. One of the strongest independent risk factors for heart attack and stroke.

Think of it as: "A genetic risk factor you can only find with a blood test."

Apo A-1

Measures HDL function — your body's ability to remove cholesterol from artery walls. The cleanup crew.

Think of it as: "How good your body is at cleaning up the cholesterol."
