How To Inject

Daily Injection — About 30 Seconds.

1
Prep
Wash your hands. Clean the injection site with an alcohol pad. Pull the syringe from the fridge a few minutes early to let it reach room temperature.
2
Inject Subcutaneous
Pinch the skin on lower abdomen, upper thigh, or back of arm. Insert at a 45° angle just under the skin — not into muscle. Push slow.
3
Rotate Sites
Different spot each day. Rotating prevents irritation and keeps absorption consistent. Most patients alternate abdomen → thigh → arm.
4
Dispose Safely
Each syringe is single-use. Do not reuse or recap. Drop in a sharps container or bring used syringes back to the clinic.

Safety

Important Safety Information.

Do Not Use If You Are:
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Living with active cancer
  • Managing serious liver, kidney, or heart conditions
  • Allergic to any ingredient in the formulation
Possible Mild Side Effects:
  • Mild redness, swelling, or itching at injection site
  • Slight headache or fatigue in the first few days
  • Occasional mild nausea or lightheadedness
Usually short-lived. Stop and contact us if symptoms are moderate or severe.
