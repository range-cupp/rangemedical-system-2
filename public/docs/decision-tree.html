Patient Routing Decision Tree — Staff Operating Guide
1 Ask 3 Routing Questions
Q1
"Is this mainly an injury / recovery issue, or energy / hormones / weight / overall health?"
This determines Front Door 1 vs Front Door 2.
Q2
"Are you open to doing labs to see what's going on under the hood?"
Only relevant for Front Door 2 patients. Injury patients skip labs.
Q3
"On a scale of 1–10, how serious are you about fixing this over the next 90 days?"
8+ = push 3-month commitment  •  Below 8 = let them start smaller
2 Route to the Right Front Door
Front Door 1 — Injury / Recovery

No labs. No assessment. Start today.

Same-day provider visit to evaluate injury and prescribe peptide protocol.

Default offer: Recovery membership — $499/mo, 3 months

Backup: Single 30-day peptide round — $675 (if they won't commit)

Say This
"For injuries, we can start you today. Most people do our Recovery program at $499/month for 3 months instead of paying $675 each month as one-offs. Same protocol, lower cost, and most plans are 8–12 weeks anyway. Want to lock that in?"

Labs only come up later if the patient asks about energy, hormones, or optimization.

Front Door 2 — Labs + Consultation

Step A — Day One: Sell the Labs

Patient pays for their lab panel (Essential $350 or Elite $750) and fills out a symptoms questionnaire. Labs are drawn this week.

No membership talk. No program talk. Just labs.

Say This
"Step one is getting your labs done so we can see exactly what's going on. We have two panels — Essential at $350 covers the core markers, or Elite at $750 for the full picture. We'll get your blood drawn this week, and when results are back, you'll sit down with the provider to go over everything and build your plan."

Step B — Results Back: 1-on-1 Provider Consultation

Provider reviews lab results with the patient, builds the clinical plan, and ends with: "Here's what we're going to do for the next 3 months."

No pricing in the consultation room. Front desk packages the plan after.

3 Match the Plan to a Lane

After the provider consultation, front desk uses this matrix to package the plan.
