subject: Why you're up at 3am.
---

If you wake up between 2 and 4am, can't fall back asleep, and lay there debating whether to grab your phone or just stare at the ceiling — your cortisol is jacked.

Cortisol is a stress hormone. It's supposed to be high in the morning (so you can get up and do things) and low at night (so you can sleep). When it's chronically inverted — high at night, flat in the morning — you wake up at 3am wired and wake up at 7am exhausted.

Why does it get inverted? A few greatest hits:

Drinking after 7pm. Especially the third glass.

Eating dinner at 9pm because that's when the kids finally went to bed.

Working until you fall into bed.

Twenty years of running on adrenaline because that's how you built your business.

You don't fix this with melatonin. Melatonin is for jet lag, not for a guy whose nervous system has been getting beaten with a 2x4 since 2003.

You fix it with: a hard cutoff on alcohol 3 hours before bed, dinner finished by 7pm if humanly possible, magnesium glycinate (400mg, an hour before sleep — not the citrate kind unless you want to spend the night in the bathroom), and 10 minutes of nothing — no phone, no TV, no thinking — before you lay down.

That's it. That's the protocol. Most guys feel a difference in 4-5 nights.
